Performance Marketing vs Influencer Marketing — Which One Do You Need?

ScaleBiz Academy · 6 min read · June 2026

If you're building a brand and you have a marketing budget, this question comes up fast: do I run paid ads, or do I work with influencers?

The honest answer is — it depends. But here's a framework to decide.

What is Performance Marketing?

Performance marketing is paid advertising where you only pay for measurable results — clicks, leads, purchases. Think Meta Ads, Google Ads, YouTube Ads. You set a budget, run ads, and track exactly what you got in return.

  • Pros: Measurable, scalable, fast results, full control
  • Cons: Requires budget upfront, can feel impersonal, ad fatigue is real

What is Influencer Marketing?

Influencer marketing is paying creators — on Instagram, YouTube, or anywhere — to promote your product to their audience. You're borrowing trust that they've already built.

  • Pros: High trust, feels authentic, builds brand awareness fast
  • Cons: Harder to measure directly, results vary wildly, finding the right creator takes work

Which should you start with?

Use this simple decision tree:

  • Do you have a proven product with good reviews? → Start with Performance Marketing. You know it converts, now scale it.
  • Is your product new and needs credibility? → Start with Influencer Marketing. Build trust first, then scale with ads.
  • Is your audience on social media and responds to peer recommendations? → Influencer Marketing first.
  • Do you need fast, measurable revenue this month? → Performance Marketing.

The best brands do both

The most effective marketing strategy combines both. Influencers build awareness and trust. Performance ads capture that demand and convert it. They feed each other — influencer content becomes ad creative, and ads retarget people who saw the influencer post.

You don't have to choose forever. Start with one, learn it deeply, then add the other when you're ready.
